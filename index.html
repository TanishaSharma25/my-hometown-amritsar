ABOUT AMRITSAR

Amritsar is a city of great virtue where the extraordinary past has influenced the present. By honouring the bravery and difficulty of its predecessors, this holy city shows its allegiance to their sacrifices. The city is renowned for its thriving, varied culture, magnificent scenery, rich soil, and delectable cuisine. Northern Punjab state in northwest India is where Amritsar City is located. It is located around 25 kilometres (15 miles) east of the Pakistani border. The city serves as both the administrative hub for the Amritsar district in the Punjabi state and the holiest place for Sikhs.

What is the best time to visit Amritsar?

The best months to visit Amritsar are September through December and February through March since the weather is still pleasant and ideal for touring. In January, the temperature drops to roughly 5°C, while in May and June, the height of summer, it can reach as high as 40°C. During the height of the monsoon in July and August, the majority of the cityscape is covered with lush vegetation. The humidity is high and the temperature is still moderate to warm. The monsoons can be avoided because they might make touring a little difficult.
